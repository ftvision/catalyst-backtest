# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Strategy authors use Catalyst Backtest while validating a runnable Catalyst graph before live execution. They arrive with a graph authored elsewhere, usually focused on whether the strategy outcome is trustworthy enough to iterate or ship.

## Product Purpose

Catalyst Backtest is a graph-driven crypto backtesting workbench. It accepts a runnable graph, a time range, an initial portfolio, and explicit simulation assumptions, then returns performance, trace events, data coverage, and final portfolio state. Success means a strategy author can answer: would this graph have made money, why did it behave that way, and which assumptions affect trust in the result.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, auditable. The interface should feel like an analytical instrument: clear enough for repeated use, explicit about caveats, and restrained enough that results and assumptions stay central.

## Anti-references

Do not make this a crypto exchange, live trading terminal, generic SaaS dashboard, graph-canvas builder, or marketing landing page. Avoid order books, news widgets, neon-on-black crypto styling, decorative metric mosaics, and freeform graph editing in v1.

## Design Principles

- Outcome first, explanation one click away.
- Make assumptions visible before and after every run.
- Keep graph context compact; do not turn the workbench into a graph editor.
- Treat missing data, rejected actions, and policy reasons as first-class product content.
- Favor dense, scannable product UI over ornamental layout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-operable controls. Use semantic landmarks, visible focus states, non-color status cues, readable fixed type scales, and reduced-motion support for product interactions.
